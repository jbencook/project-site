Bayesian logistic regression

The Adult Data Set is commonly used to benchmark machine learning algorithms. The goal is to use demographic features, or variables, to predict whether an individual makes more than $50,000 per year. The data set is almost 20 years old, and therefore, not perfect for determining the probability that I will earn more than $50K, but it is a nice, simple dataset that can be used to showcase a few benefits of using Bayesian logistic regression over its frequentist counterpart.

Social network dynamics

Strong social ties like friendships and marriages tend to exist between people who are similar to one another. It is true for unchanging characteristics, such as race and gender; and it is true for attributes over which we have control, such as political orientation and religion. This pattern is typically referred to as homophily (in my thesis I use the term “network autocorrelation” which is less ambiguous, but more cumbersome). Homophily arises because a) people prefer to form relationships with people who are similar to themselves, a mechanism called selection, and/or b) people become more similar to their friends over time, a mechanism called influence.

Scripting for success in Hollywood

Is it possible to predict box office success with only a movie script? In this project for CS109: Data Science, we decided to find out. Using a Latent Dirichlet Allocation model to identify prevalent topics, we were able to predict box office success with reasonable accuracy.
